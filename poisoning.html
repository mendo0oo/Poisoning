Poison Hybrid

They collect. We contaminate. Poison Hybrid is my browser privacy project for blocking trackers, killing sketchy redirects, reducing fingerprint leaks, and making tracker data less clean than collectors want it to be.

Open repo Contact

What it is

Poison Hybrid is a Firefox-first privacy extension with a hybrid blocking backend and my own page-level privacy modules on top. It blocks tracker and ad requests, hardens against popup traps and redirect chains, spoofs common fingerprint surfaces, shields WebRTC/header leaks, rejects common cookie banners, and can add controlled fake noise to tracker-style collection endpoints.

Why I care

The modern web treats normal browsing like raw material. Clicks, scrolls, searches, devices, embeds, misclicks, and little browser quirks get measured, packaged, and used for profiles. I do not want every movement online to become a clean signal for ad companies, analytics vendors, or data brokers. If data is the fuel, Poison throws sand into the engine.

Core ideas

  • Block the obvious trackers, ads, popups, fake dialogs, blank tabs, and redirect bait.
  • Keep visible user-clicked download links working instead of blocking everything blindly.
  • Spoof noisy browser surfaces like navigator data, screen values, canvas, WebGL, plugins, media devices, and WebRTC candidates.
  • Use temporary poison profiles so fake tracker-noise personas reset when the browser session ends.
  • Keep Antidote Mode for Cloudflare, banking, login pages, video embeds, and fragile sites.
  • Treat privacy as active resistance, not a checkbox hidden three menus deep.

Boundaries

Poison improves privacy, but it is not invisibility. It does not hide an IP address, bypass Cloudflare or VPN reputation checks, protect logged-in accounts from first-party tracking, or replace a hardened browser profile, VPN/Tor, and account separation. It is a defensive privacy tool and a learning project, not a promise that the whole internet suddenly forgets who you are.

Browser stance

Firefox and Firefox-based browsers are the main target because they still give privacy extensions stronger control. Brave is supported as the practical Chromium option. Chrome gets a compatibility build because Manifest V3 limits what blockers can do. Opera, Opera GX, and telemetry-heavy Chromium reskins are not the focus. A gaming skin, sidebar, wallet, or AI button does not turn surveillance infrastructure into privacy software.

Status

The working build is Poison Hybrid: Firefox XPI, Brave ZIP, and a weaker Chrome compatibility ZIP. It is still a work in progress. Expect broken sites, rough edges, and settings that need tuning. That is the cost of building something that pushes back instead of politely accepting every tracker script the web throws at the browser.
